Abnormal Corn Ears

Nitrogen and phosphorus uptake are rapid during silking through the dough stage. Nutrient concentrations in the plant are highly correlated to final grain yield and response to previously applied fertilizer can be seen. Nutrient deficiencies occurring during grain fill commonly result in unfilled kernels or chaffy ears.

  • Poor Pollination at Ear Tip

    Symptoms

    Cob tissue without kernels on the last one or more inches of the ear tip. Ovules not fertilized at ear tip.

    Causes

    Poor fertilization of ear tip ovules at silking. Same as those for Poor, Incomplete Kernel Set.
