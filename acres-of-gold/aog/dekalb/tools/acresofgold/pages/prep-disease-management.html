Disease Management

Seedling Disease Treatment

Cool soil conditions (<50 to 55o F) can delay corn germination, emergence and predispose corn seedlings to diseases. Seedling susceptibility to diseases increases with the length of time a seed sits in the ground and with increasing levels of stress during germination.

Seedling diseases can occur in the form of seed rots, seedling blights, and/or root rots.

  • If the above-ground symptoms are due to seedling diseases, mesocotyls and/or roots will also by symptomatic

Disease inoculum can build in continuous corn cropping systems; however, some seedling diseases infect both corn and soybeans.
