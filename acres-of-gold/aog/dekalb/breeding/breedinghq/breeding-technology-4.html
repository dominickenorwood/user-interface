The Benefits of Seed Chipping

Before Monsanto's invention of the seed chipper, identifying seeds with specific genes required growing plants from the seeds, collecting leaf samples from the plants, and then testing the leaf samples for DNA markers. Based on the results from the lab, unwanted plants were excluded from the breeding program. By changing the focus from leaf to seed, the seed chipper allows us to avoid growing and testing unwanted plants.

The seed chipper allows us to identify seeds containing the best combination of genes, in a fraction of the time and at a fraction of the cost of leaf sampling. In addition, DNA marker selection using seed-chipping technology cuts years from our delivery of improved products to farmers. Only those seeds that have the best gene combinations are planted in the field for further testing and to grow into the next generation of elite breeding products.
