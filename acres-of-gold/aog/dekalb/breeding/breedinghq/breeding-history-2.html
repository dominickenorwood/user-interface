The Basic Principle of Breeding Select The Best, Throw Away The Rest

In the breeding process, genes from parent plants are shuffled into a multitude of combinations as crosses and new plants are created. Breeders work to make many different crosses, identify the plants with the best combinations of genes, and then use them to develop future products.

Through the DEKALB® brand integrated approach to breeding, which includes access to Monsanto's GlobalBreeding Organization, vast testing network, and massive global germplasm pool, we bring the best product choices available to every farm. DEKALB® products are developed and selected through a massive breeding program that is unmatched by anyone in the industry, providing superior agronomics, plant health and yield potential. In fact, over 99% of the plants produced in the breeding program don't meet the standards to become a new DEKALB product, and we throw them away.
