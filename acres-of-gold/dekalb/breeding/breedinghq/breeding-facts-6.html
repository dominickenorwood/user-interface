To get the right combination of 20 different genes into a single plant, you would need to create a trillion corn plants. This is approximately how many corn plants there are in the Corn Belt each year. So, you would have to walk from Nebraska to Ohio, looking at every corn plant, and know what you were looking for, to find one plant that had the right combination of genes.

We can use molecular breeding to decrease the odds of finding it from 1 in a trillion to 1 in 5.
