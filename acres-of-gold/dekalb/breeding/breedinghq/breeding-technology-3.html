Double Haploid TechnologyUnderstanding the Genetic Game Changer

The first step in making a new corn hybrid is crossing two parents that are inbred for all of their genes. Traditionally, inbred plants are developed by corn breeders using an intensive process that takes several years. From these efforts, improved inbred plants are created that can go on to make the superior hybrids of the future. If the process of new inbred development could be shortened, we could bring even higher-yielding hybrids to the field faster.

DEKALBÂ® breeders now use a technology which creates "instant inbreds", called doubled haploids, which reduces the time it takes to get the next generation of inbred plants. Double haploid breeding can now accomplish in one year what used to take 8-9 generations using traditional methods.
