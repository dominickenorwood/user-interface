Where Breeding Meets Biotech

At the Gene Transformation Labs in Mystic, Connecticut, scientists use a sophisticated testing process to advance traits and minimize growing challenges. Breeders at this premier agricultural biotech research center are focused on two primary areas within the DEKALB product portfolio: world-class corn transgenic pipeline and drought-tolerant technologies.

With more than 100 research scientists, the nearly 80,000-square-foot facility includes over 20,000 square feet of greenhouses situated on 16 acres of land. The Mystic facility is divided into key functional areas that manage the entire process from initial plant transformation to growth in the greenhouse. These teams process thousands of plants annually, with a highly controlled barcode management system to ensure accuracy and efficiency.

Mystic's research teams have used advanced scientific tools to introduce genes and develop high-performance products that have allowed farmers the opportunity to be more successful on their farms.
