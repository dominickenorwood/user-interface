Optimizing Corn Yield Potential

Optimizing corn yield potential requires a balanced management approach that strives to maximize corn yield in favorable conditions while managing weather-associated risks. The table below shows the hierarchy of "corn yield wonders" as established by the research of Dr. Fred Below with the University of Illinois. In his research "weather" accounts for 27% of total yield potential.

7 Wonders of Corn Yield
Weather
Nitrogen
Hybrid
Previous Crop
Plant Population
Tillage
Growth Regulators

Weather can both directly and indirectly impact corn production and may affect corn-on-corn relative to corn following soybean differently.

Impacts include:

  • Timing and effectiveness of tillage
  • Residue breakdown
  • Nutrient availability (N cycle — applied vs. available)
  • Disease incidence and severity
  • Root establishment and architecture
