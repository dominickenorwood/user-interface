Planting Population

Average corn populations have increased by 300 to 425 plants per acre per year in the Corn Belt over the past 25 years. These increases have been directly correlated to increases in corn yield on an annual basis; however, achieving the optimum seeding rate for a given field is complex and is affected by both economics and final stand yield components.

More Plants = More Ears

Yield increases with modern products are related to the ability of plants to withstand stress and produce an ear on every plant. Thus, more plants per acre equals more ears per acre.

Population Recommendations

Great efforts are spent researching plant populations for specific products. After identifying products that are good fits for your area, population recommendations for each product should be carefully examined. Understanding how individual products respond to different populations can help maximize corn yield potential.

Understanding Refuge

The best way to preserve the benefits of insect protection offered by Bacillus thuringiensis (B.t.) technology is to develop and implement an Insect Resistance Management (IRM) plan. Corn refuge is a key component of an IRM plan. Refuge requirements vary by the type of corn product being planted and the location of planting.
